Before & After

The same task. Two very different experiences.

Before -- ~5 minutes per check
1. Google "Oakland recreation court booking"
2. Find the right link in the city portal
3. Log in (if you remember your password)
4. Navigate to the right facility
5. Click through the calendar date by date
6. Scan each time slot for availability
7. Write down the open times
The math: 5 min/check × 3 courts × 5 days/week × 50 weeks = 62.5 hours/year. At $25/hr, that's $1,562/year on one task.
After -- one click
The Smart Way to Build It

I had two options. I picked the free one.

The obvious approach was to use AI vision -- point a model at the city's website and have it read the screen. That works, but it costs about $0.50 every time someone checks. For a tool people use multiple times a week, that adds up fast.

Instead, I found the city's public booking widget URL and wrote a script that reads the data directly. Same result. Zero cost per use. The tool pays for itself on day one because there's nothing to pay.
