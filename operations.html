The Problem

Most "operational improvements" fail because nobody diagnoses what's actually broken.

A consultant hands you a binder. A software vendor sells you a tool. A coach tells you to delegate more. None of it sticks, because none of it was built around how your business actually leaks money, time, and customers.

Here's how it actually goes. You hired someone to get out from under the work — and you did, mostly. But you couldn't describe what they do all day. You don't know if they're efficient, or which parts of the business only work because they know how. If they left tomorrow, you'd be back in the weeds figuring out what they were doing while the business bled customers.

The business works. It works because of one person's head, not because of a system. That's the problem.

The Toolkit

What I actually install.

Seven interventions I draw from, based on what the diagnostic turns up. Most engagements use two or three. Every one of them came out of a real business I ran.

01
Funnel-Stage Diagnosis

Isolate which stage of your customer acquisition is actually leaking. Most businesses think they have a marketing problem and actually have a fulfillment-team-unconsciously-throttling-intake problem. I've seen it enough times to find it quickly.

02
Shared Scoreboards

Color-coded KPI sheets that every manager sees the same way every week. Cheapest, highest-leverage management tool I know. Pairs with a three-question dashboard: are customers happy, are employees happy, are we making money?

03
Replication-Ready Playbook

The two documents every hand-offable business needs: a Store Opener manual (how to stand up a new location from zero) and a Training Curriculum (how to bring a new hire to independent productivity).

04
Six-Stage Hand-Offability Ladder

A diagnostic framework that tells you exactly which stage of operational independence your business is at — and what the next stage requires. Most owners can't describe the ladder; they just know they're stuck on it.

05
Values as Vaccinations

Values aren't aspirations — they're protection against specific failure modes you've personally witnessed. I help you turn the five things you're most afraid of into five behavioral rules every new hire learns on day one.

06
Fear-First Customer Surfaces

Your website, intake scripts, onboarding emails — every customer-facing surface should lead with what neutralizes the customer's fear, not what the operator is proud of. Most businesses have this inverted.

How It Works

Three phases. No binders. No shelfware.

A structured 90-day engagement that ends with systems your team runs, not recommendations you have to implement yourself.

Phase 1
Diagnostic

Two to four weeks. I walk through every part of your operation and identify the real leak points — the ones costing you money, customers, and time. You get a written report with the top three interventions, ranked by impact. If the diagnostic doesn't find at least $25K of waste, you don't pay for it.

Phase 2
Build

Sixty to ninety days. I install the scoreboards, SOPs, and systems your team actually uses. Not a binder — a working set of tools and rhythms that your people help build and therefore adopt. Typical outputs: a weekly KPI dashboard, a documented process library, role clarity for your leadership team.

Phase 3
Hand Off

I'm not your permanent COO. Once the systems are running and your team owns them, I step back. Ongoing monthly retainer is available if you want me to stay involved — but the hand-off is the default, not the exception.

What it takes from you

About two hours a week of your time. That's it.

I don't need you to pause operations, reorganize your team, or sit in workshops. I work alongside your existing rhythm. What I need from you: one 30-minute check-in per week, access to your team for interviews and shadowing, and honest answers when I ask hard questions. Your team doesn't stop working. You don't stop running the business. I build the systems underneath what's already happening.
