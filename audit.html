The week you’re having

Every owner I talk to is in roughly the same place.

You read about AI every day. Your competitors are probably using it. Your kids and grandkids talk about it. Some salesperson keeps trying to sell you a tool that costs $4,000 a month and promises to change your business.

You don’t know what’s real and what’s noise. You don’t know what’s worth paying for. You don’t know what to fix first — your operations are full of things you’ve been meaning to get to for years, and now there’s a whole new category of things you don’t even understand.

So you do nothing, or you buy the wrong thing, or you sit through another sales call and feel exactly the same afterward.

What you walk away with

Three things, on paper, by the end of the week.

01

Clarity on what’s actually broken.

Not the symptoms — the real bottlenecks costing you money, time, or sleep. Named, ranked by what they cost you, in plain English.

02

A roadmap for what to fix first.

The top three to five interventions, ordered by impact and effort. For each one: what to do, who can do it, what it costs, what it saves. Real numbers.

03

Where AI fits, and where it doesn’t.

For each fix on the roadmap: is this a tool you can buy, a custom build, a process change, or something AI is genuinely the right answer for? Some things AI is great at. Some things it’s terrible at. You’ll know which is which for your specific business.

Honest answers

Questions I get before booking.

How is this different from someone selling me an AI tool? +
I don’t sell tools. I’ll recommend tools, builds, or process changes — whichever is actually right. If the answer for your business is “you don’t need AI for any of this,” I’ll tell you that.
What if my business is too small for $25,000 of waste? +
Then the audit is free, per the guarantee. If a business is under roughly $1.5M in revenue, that threshold is harder to clear. I’ll be straight on the fit call if I think your business isn’t the right size.
How much of my time will this actually take? +
60 minutes for the discovery call. 30 minutes for the review call. That’s it. Everything else happens on my side without you involved.
How long until I get the report? +
About a week from the discovery call.
Do you work with my industry? +
Probably. I’ve worked with home services, professional firms, multi-location service businesses, and creative agencies. The operations problems are mostly the same regardless of vertical. If yours is genuinely outside what I can help with, I’ll say so on the fit call.
What if I read the report and decide not to do anything? +
That’s a totally normal outcome. The audit gives you clarity. What you do with it is up to you.
Is this remote or in-person? +
Remote works fine for the audit itself. If you’re in the Bay Area and want me to walk through your operation in person, that’s available — usually adds a day to the timeline, no extra cost.
Do you sign an NDA? +
Yes. Send me one or use mine.
