The Problem

Buyers don't want to buy a job.

Most small businesses are too dependent on the owner. The owner knows how everything works, handles the important customers, makes all the decisions, and keeps the whole thing running. That works fine until it's time to sell.

When your business can't function without you, it sells for less, takes longer to close, or doesn't sell at all. Buyers discount heavily for key-person risk. The operations that felt like a strength become the thing that kills the deal.

The owners who get full price are the ones who built a business that works on its own. That's not an accident. It's a system.

What I Do

I come in and build the systems that let your business run without you.

That means documented processes, restructured roles, upgraded tools, and trained people. Everything a buyer needs to see a business that stands on its own.

01
Process Documentation

Every critical workflow written down so anyone can follow it. No more institutional knowledge locked in the owner's head.

02

03

04

About Chuck

I've built this. Then sold it.

I built DVD Your Memories from a side hustle into 27 employees across four locations in two states, then sold it. The buyer looked at more than fifty companies before choosing mine. What stood out was that the business actually worked without me. I had built the operations playbooks, the training systems, and the store-opening procedures that let new locations come online fast and run consistently. That's the book: Startup to Sold, published by Inc. Magazine Imprints in 2023.

The systems I built are the reason the company was sellable. And that pattern has repeated in everything I've done since.

As COO of a food and beverage startup, I tripled revenue and built their first ERP system from scratch to manage a perishable supply chain and scale production. I built and sold a media company in the electric scooter space, where the systems were solid enough that the business transferred cleanly. And I built a two-sided marketplace from scratch (payments, SMS, custom database) that now runs 25,000 monthly users on its own.
