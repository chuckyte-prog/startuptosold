How is this even possible?

The short version, since I know you'll wonder.

1

The AI got tools.

A few years ago, AI could only chat. Now it can read my email, search the web, write files, build websites, and check its own work. It operates a computer the way a person does.

2

The AI got skills.

I can teach it how I like things done by writing simple notes in plain English. It remembers them forever. So it knows my voice, my preferences, my projects. Like training a new assistant once and never having to repeat yourself.

3

The AI got a memory.

It remembers what we've worked on across days, weeks, months. New conversations don't start from zero. It actually knows me at this point.

4

The AI is now writing the next AI.

This is the wild part. The engineers at Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google all use AI to build the next version of AI. Each generation builds the one after it. That's why progress is moving so fast. It's compounding.

What I actually want to do with all this.

This part isn't about showing off. It's about why I've been heads-down learning all of it for the last year.

Big tech companies already have this. They have armies of engineers building these systems for themselves. The local businesses don't. The dentist on Tracy Boulevard, the moving company down the road, the bookkeeper with 40 small clients, the law firm with three partners. They're all still doing things by hand that AI could be doing for them in seconds.

That's the work I want. I want to walk into a small business and say: "Show me the thing you do every week that you hate doing." Then build the AI that does it for them, while they go run their actual business.

For example

Imagine your Seder, but for a business.

Think about what you do every Passover. You send the invite to 22 people. You track who's coming. You assign who brings matzah, who brings wine, who brings the salad. You answer the same questions five times. You update the headcount. You text the people who haven't replied. You make a kid-friendly Haggadah by hand because nobody's selling the one you want.

Now picture a small business owner doing that every single day. Customers instead of family. Estimates instead of side dishes. Follow-ups instead of RSVPs. Same exhausting coordination, but for their livelihood.

I'd build the AI that handles all of it for them. Answers the questions, drafts the proposals, follows up with the no-replies, keeps the running list. So the owner can do the part that actually needs them. Same way you'd want help with the Seder so you could just enjoy your family.

The startup I sold (DVD Your Memories, the one in my book) ran on me doing all of that by hand for years. If I'd had what I have now back then, I'd have gotten my evenings back a decade earlier.

So that's what I'm building toward. Helping small business owners get their evenings back. That's the dream.
